English

3 Topics

Reading Skills

Students develop close reading, inference, and comprehension through prose, poetry, and nonfiction. They learn to identify main ideas, analyse language choices, and respond thoughtfully to different kinds of texts.

Writing Techniques

English writing focuses on structure, grammar, punctuation, and vocabulary. Learners practise descriptive, narrative, and argumentative writing so they can communicate clearly for academic and real-life situations.

Speaking & Listening

This area builds confidence in presentation, discussion, and spoken communication. Students learn to express ideas fluently, listen actively, and adjust tone and language for different audiences.

Mathematics

3 Topics

Algebra

Algebra introduces expressions, equations, graphs, and functions. Students learn to solve problems systematically, recognise patterns, and apply symbolic methods with confidence and accuracy.

Geometry & Measure

This topic includes angles, shapes, area, volume, transformations, and mensuration. Learners strengthen spatial reasoning and apply formulas in both abstract questions and real-world contexts.

Statistics & Probability

Students interpret data, calculate averages, and evaluate chance using charts, tables, and probability models. The topic supports logical thinking and evidence-based decision-making.

Science

3 Topics

Biology

Biology explores living organisms, cells, ecosystems, and body systems. Students study how life works and how organisms interact with one another and their environment.

Chemistry

This topic focuses on matter, atoms, bonding, reactions, and calculations. Learners investigate substances and changes through explanation, observation, and experimental evidence.

Physics

Physics introduces forces, motion, energy, electricity, waves, and space. Students learn how scientific laws explain natural phenomena and support modern technology.

Business Studies

3 Topics

Enterprise

Students discover how businesses begin, identify market needs, and shape ideas into useful products or services. This area encourages initiative, planning, and entrepreneurial thinking.

Marketing

Marketing explains how businesses attract and retain customers using product design, pricing, promotion, and distribution. Learners analyse target markets and customer expectations.

Operations & People

This section looks at production, human resources, customer service, and organisation. Students understand how effective management supports growth and long-term performance.

Economics

3 Topics

Basic Economic Problem

Learners explore scarcity, choice, and opportunity cost. They understand why resources are limited and how individuals, firms, and governments make decisions.

Markets

This topic explains demand, supply, pricing, and competition. Students examine how market behaviour shapes outcomes for both consumers and producers.

National Economy

Students study inflation, unemployment, growth, and government policy. The subject builds an understanding of how countries manage performance and development.

Accounting

3 Topics

Bookkeeping

Students record transactions using journals, ledgers, and trial balances. This topic builds the foundation for accuracy, consistency, and organised financial records.

Financial Statements

Learners prepare income statements and statements of financial position. They understand how businesses report performance and financial health clearly.

Analysis & Evaluation

This area develops the ability to interpret accounting data using ratios and comparisons. Students assess profitability, liquidity, and efficiency for better decisions.

ICT

3 Topics

Digital Systems

Students learn how computers process, store, and present information. This topic introduces hardware, software, and the essential role of digital systems in modern study and work.

Networks & Communication

ICT networking explains the internet, online communication, security, and file sharing. Learners understand how data travels and how safe digital behaviour protects users.

Applications of ICT

This area focuses on spreadsheets, databases, presentations, and word processing. Students apply digital tools to organise information and improve productivity.

Computer Science

3 Topics

Problem Solving

Students break problems into steps, design algorithms, and use pseudocode or flowcharts. This topic builds logical thinking and supports efficient computational solutions.

Programming

Programming introduces sequence, selection, iteration, variables, and arrays. Learners write, test, and refine code while developing structured problem-solving habits.

Data Representation

This topic explains binary, logic, storage, and computer architecture. Students understand how machines handle data internally and why these ideas matter in computing.
